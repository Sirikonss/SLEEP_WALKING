INSOMNIA

Source from MedicalNewsToday

Insomnia is a sleep disorder that regularly affects millions of people worldwide. In short, individuals with insomnia find it difficult to fall asleep or stay asleep. The effects can be devastating.

Facts on insomnia:

According to the National Sleep Foundation, 30-40 percent of American adults report that they have had symptoms of insomnia within the last 12 months, and 10-15 percent of adults claim to have chronic insomnia.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Difficulty falling asleep at night.
  • Waking during the night.
  • Waking earlier than desired.
  • WakingStill feeling tired after a night's sleep.
  • WakingDaytime fatigue or sleepiness.
  • WakingIrritability, depression, or anxiety.
  • WakingPoor concentration and focus.
  • WakingBeing uncoordinated, an increase in errors or accidents.
  • WakingTension headaches (feels like a tight band around head).
  • WakingDifficulty socializing.
  • WakingGastrointestinal symptoms.
  • WakingWorrying about sleeping.

TYPES

Transient insomnia - occurs when symptoms last up to three nights.

Acute insomnia - also called short-term insomnia. Symptoms persist for several weeks.

Chronic insomnia - this type lasts for months, and sometimes years. According to the National Institutes of Health, the majority of chronic insomnia cases are side effects resulting from another primary problem.
